scenario: Acne in teenager, first-line treatment
category: Derm
density: Sparse

Doctor: Hey Jayden, how's it going? What brings you in?

Patient: Um, my mom made me come in because of my face. The acne.

Doctor: Okay. How long has it been an issue?

Patient: I don't know, like since the beginning of the school year? So maybe six months.

Doctor: Let me take a look. Yeah, I see some whiteheads and blackheads mostly on the forehead and cheeks, and a few inflamed red bumps along the jawline. Any on your chest or back?

Patient: A little on my back.

Doctor: Have you tried anything for it so far?

Patient: I wash my face with that, um, Clearasil stuff. It doesn't really help though.

Doctor: Alright. So here's what I'd recommend. Two things. First, a benzoyl peroxide wash — 5 percent is a good starting strength. Use it once a day, at night. Lather it on, let it sit for a minute or two, then rinse off. Second, a topical retinoid — I'm going to prescribe adapalene gel 0.1 percent. You apply a thin layer to your whole face at bedtime after the wash, once your skin is dry.

Patient: Both every night?

Doctor: Yep. Wash first, wait a few minutes, then the adapalene. Now here's the thing — your skin might get a little dry and irritated at first, especially the first couple weeks. That's normal. Use a gentle, oil-free moisturizer if that happens. And sunscreen during the day because the retinoid makes your skin more sensitive to sun.

Patient: How long until it actually works?

Doctor: This is the hard part. Give it at least six to eight weeks before you judge it. Acne treatments are slow. It might even look a little worse before it gets better — that's called purging and it's temporary.

Patient: That kinda sucks.

Doctor: I know. But stick with it. If we're not seeing improvement in two months, we have next steps. Sound good?

Patient: Yeah, okay.

Doctor: And one more thing — don't pick at them. I know it's tempting. But it makes scarring worse. Alright?

Patient: Fine.

Doctor: Cool. I'll see you in about eight weeks to check progress. You got this.
